##  WORK DONE  
This week, our team focused on understanding real-world challenges in insurance claim processing, particularly in rural and small hospital environments. We analyzed how claims are currently handled using paper-based systems and identified major issues such as delays, manual errors, lack of transparency, and absence of prioritization for urgent cases.

Customer interactions were led by **Yeshvanth T S**, who coordinated with different stakeholders and collected meaningful insights. The team was divided to gather perspectives from hospital insurance agents, insurance advisors, hospital staff, and patients. Parallelly, documentation and presentation work was handled to organize the collected information effectively.

---

##  CUSTOMER PERSONAS  

**1. Sathvik (Hospital Insurance Agent)**  
Works inside a hospital and assists patients in submitting claims. He faces challenges such as handling multiple claims at once, verifying handwritten documents, and manually identifying urgent cases. This makes the process slow and error-prone.

**2. Surya Narayana Reddy (Insurance Advisor / Team Lead)**  
Leads a team that helps customers choose policies and manage claims. He faces issues like claim delays, repeated documentation, and maintaining customer trust. Lack of a structured system affects both efficiency and credibility.

**3. Varun (Hospital Billing Staff)**  
Responsible for billing and maintaining patient records. He deals with manual data entry, duplicate work, and frequent errors due to unstructured documents. There is also no proper way to prioritize critical cases.

**4. Hanish (Patient’s Caregiver)**  
Represents a patient’s family perspective. He experienced stress due to long waiting times, lack of clear communication, and repeated document submissions during the claim process.

---

##  ANALYSIS & INSIGHTS  
From the collected feedback, the team identified several key insights:  
- Manual paperwork is a major bottleneck in claim processing  
- Lack of prioritization delays critical cases  
- Unstructured and handwritten data increases processing time  
- Poor communication leads to confusion and stress for patients  

These insights helped us clearly understand the problem and guided the design of our AI-based solution.
